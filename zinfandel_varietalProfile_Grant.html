Zinfandel — Varietal Profile

Overview

Zinfandel is a bold, full-bodied red grape most famously grown in California. Known for its ripe, jammy fruit character, high alcohol, and spicy edge, it can range from bright and fruit-forward “zin” styles to intense, concentrated old-vine expressions with serious structure.

Core Characteristics

  • Primary Flavors: Blackberry, raspberry jam, black cherry, black pepper, cinnamon, clove, raisin, smoke
  • Body: Medium-plus to full
  • Tannin: Medium to medium-high
  • Acidity: Medium to medium-low
  • Sweetness: Dry (classic) to off-dry (White Zinfandel blush)

Aroma & Flavor

Aroma Profile: Intense ripe black and red berries, brambly fruit, baking spices, and often a distinctive cracked black pepper note.

Flavor Profile: Juicy, jammy fruit explosion up front, followed by spice and sometimes a subtle smoky or chocolate edge; old-vine versions add earthy depth and firmer tannins.

Origin & Style

Common Regions: California (Lodi, Sonoma Dry Creek Valley, Paso Robles, Sierra Foothills old vines); Croatia (original home as Crljenak Kaštelanski/Tribidrag); Puglia, Italy (as Primitivo)

Winemaking Notes: Thrives in warm climates; old vines (50–100+ years) produce concentrated, balanced wines. Styles vary from lighter, fresher versions to big, high-alcohol (15–16%) powerhouses.

Pairing & Service

Best With: Barbecue (ribs, brisket), smoked or grilled meats, spicy sausages, turkey with cranberry, pizza, burgers, dark chocolate, blue cheese

Serve Temperature: 60–65°F (slightly cooler for fresher styles)

Decant Time: 30–60 minutes for younger, bolder bottles

Aging Potential: 5–15 years for top old-vine examples; most best within 3–7 years

Vibes / Personality

Exuberant, generous, and unpretentious—big-hearted and full of life, the friend who turns every gathering into a celebration.

Personal Notes

A wine that doesn’t hold back; it invites you to relax, laugh, and enjoy the moment without overthinking.
